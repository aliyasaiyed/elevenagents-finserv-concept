# ElevenAgents for Financial Services — Positioning Concept

An unsolicited spec concept I built for ElevenLabs' Agents platform: a positioning angle and landing page for taking conversational AI agents to enterprise financial-services buyers.

**[View the live page →](https://aliyasaiyed.github.io/elevenagents-finserv-concept/)**

## Why this vertical

The ElevenAgents promise is to end "waiting on hold." That pain is most acute and most expensive in banking, lending, and insurance, where people call at high-stakes moments (a declined card, a missed payment, suspected fraud) and every second on hold turns into churn, complaints, and compliance exposure. It's also a vertical where the buyer's first question is whether the product is safe and compliant, which makes it a good test of positioning, not just copy.

## The positioning thinking

- The hero sells the outcome (answer on the first ring), not the technology.
- A "cost of hold" section sets the stakes before the product appears.
- "How it works" pre-empts the "this is just another chatbot" objection by leading with reliability, integration, and governance.
- Use cases narrow to one high-volume queue you can land and expand from, which is how enterprise adoption actually sequences.
- Security gets its own section, because in financial services it gates the deal rather than being a footnote.
